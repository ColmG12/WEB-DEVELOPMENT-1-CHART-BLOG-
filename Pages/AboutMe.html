Colm G - About me

Hello!

My name is Colm Gallagher. I grew up in London listening to UK radio for every hour I possibly could, with Martin Collins, Neil Fox & others being my inspirations on Capital FM, before I became hooked on Chris Evans & Chris Moyles soon after.

After a stint in hospital radio I transferred to becoming a full-time club DJ. Between 1996-2010 I held various London resedencies including:

  • "The Swan"
  • "The Venue"
  • "O'Neill's"
  • "The Vale Bar"
  • "The Borderline"

The Swan is simply one of the most famous Irish clubs in London. Having hosted the likes of "The Pogues", "The Stunning" and "The Fureys", amongst many others, you haven't lived if you've never experienced a night in Stockwell's iconic hotspot!

The Venue, located in New Cross, a 7 storey superclub which hosted Oasis, Blur, Pulp etc before they hit the big time!

O'Neill's is London's largest chain of Irish theme bars. I was the resident DJ in Leytonstone O'Neill's for 4 years playing to roughly 1000 people every Friday & Saturday night.

In 1999 I was tasked with a new challenge by the new owners of The Vale Bar in trendy East Dulwich.....Start from scratch! Over 5 years we went from nothing to a full 400 capacity every weekend.

The Borderline is a top club in London's West End supplying dance music to a mix of locals and tourists.

As well as the above, I played the World famous "Cafe Royal" in Regent Street on behalf of the Irish Youth Foundation, and some ITV backstage parties.

My passion for radio became reignited upon relocating to Ireland and taking a course at Radio Kerry Training.

I presented a weekly show called '60s@6' from 2014 to Feb 2017 and was also the main cover presenter for 'Powerplay', 'The Afternoon Show' & 'The Hitlist' on Shannonside Northern Sound, broadcasting across the counties of Longford, Roscommon, Leitrim, Cavan and Monaghan.

As well as radio, my other big passion (for obvious reasons), is music. I've been collecting a huge database in my hope of eventually owning every song that has ever officialy charted! I plan to list and blog about my top 100 songs of every year since the official UK charts commenced in 1952. Hopefully you will enjoy this site and tell your friends about it too!

Thanks!
