Number 9

Peak position: 4

Weeks on chart: 14

It’s quite astonishing that it has taken TWENTY FIVE YEARS for Chuck D & Flavor Flav & the crew to finally break the UK top ten! The kings of the classic sample hook struck gold again with this tune by going full circle and actually sampling their first release, the 1987 hit, “Public Enemy No.1”. Originally released for Public Enemy’s 20th anniversary in 2007, "Harder Than You Think" finally got the success it deserved after Channel 4 used it as the theme for their Paralympics coverage.

Number 2

Rudimental ft John Newman - "Feel The Love"

Peak position: 1

Weeks on chart: 67

As someone who spent many a night back in the late 90’s frequenting Mass in Brixton (And I ain’t talking church as Sammy Yousef will testify!), it’s strange to think that it took over another decade before a drum & bass tune topped the UK charts. DJ Fresh’s “Hot Right Now” was the first to do so, but "Feel The Love" from Hackney based Rudimental was the finest d&b effort of the year. John Newman supplies one of the best vocals of 2012 as the track slowly builds from a dubstep bassline into a huge crescendo of drum & bass mixed with jazzy horns. The timing of the breakdown is spot on and the track rises once again as Newman is joined by a gleeful backing chorus, and finally into a rousing blast of chorus before ending with the silky smooth soul tones of Newman’s acapella……..TUUUUUUUUNAGE!

Number 1

Labrinth ft Emeli Sandé - "Beneath Your Beautiful"

Peak position: 1

Weeks on chart: 31

It’s not very often that I love a song instantly upon hearing it. "Beneath Your Beautiful" was one of those exceptions. I remember Dermot O’Leary mentioning Labrinth coming up after the break on X-Factor and not really having much excitement. Don’t get me wrong, I’m a big fan of Lab, but I was not expecting to see him sat down in front of a piano chiming a beautiful set of opening chords. Gary Barlow was sitting in the judges chair probably wishing he had written it. I was hooked right there and then as he sang the haunting first verse all the way up to the first “Would you let me…..” (56 secs in), where out of nowhere he seems to be channelling the great Ray Charles. A vocal master class only aided by the gorgeous voice of Emeli Sandé once the thumping bass kicks in. An epic duet all the way through with Emeli hitting the pitch perfect “See beneath your beautiful…” (3.55), and the two of them ‘Taking it home’ as they say before Lab ends on those wonderful final chords. A worthy song to top any years chart.
